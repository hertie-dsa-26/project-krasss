Key patterns in the data

Preliminary findings · for discussion
🌡️
Heat and sleep quality
Counties with more days above 90°F (DX90) show higher rates of poor sleep. The effect is strongest in lower-income counties with less air conditioning access.
↑ Higher sleep problems in hot counties
🧠
Demographics and mental distress
Counties with higher proportions of residents without a high school degree show consistently higher mental distress rates, independent of temperature effects.
Strong correlation with education levels
❄️
Cold extremes and physical health
Extreme cold events (EMNT) correlate with spikes in physical distress (PHLTH) and COPD rates, clearest in northern counties.
Signal strongest above 45°N latitude
📈
Rising trends 2013–2023
Average mental distress has risen across most counties over the decade. This trend appears faster in counties that also saw rising average max temperatures.
Upward trend across all health outcomes
